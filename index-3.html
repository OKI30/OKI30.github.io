About Printing Technology

Printing color

Printing or printing color slant is the most troublesome problem when designers output, we see the print is composed of light, paper and ink. Monochrome printing is used when budgets are tight or when specific colors are used. For example, the use of specific Pantone colors in many brands or companies' printed works can be very effective in maintaining color uniformity to ensure brand color awareness. It is recommended to buy a copy of Pantone chromatograph and choose the corresponding color number for printing.

Two and three color printing allows pantone color to be mixed. Overprinting two transparent inks produces a third color. Printing black and white images in two colors and three colors enhances the color effect.Four-color printing, known as CMYK, is cyan, magenta, yellow and black, and can be used to create countless colors according to ink proportions. That's what we see most often today.

Printing color

Pantone-color Matching System(PMS)

Pantone, also known as spot color, has many colors to choose from, but we know that colors can vary from paper to paper. In order to make the color closer to the design, PMS color sample has many versions for different paper, such as matte paper and copper plate. In this guide, a pantone code ending in C indicates colors that match only glossy coated paper. A pantone code ending in "U" represents a print color that matches the matte paper.

Printing paper

The choice of paper is absolutely a part that needs to be taken into consideration in the design process. The thickness, texture and color of the paper will affect the feelings conveyed by the design image. In the process of making a sample, you can choose several kinds of paper to ensure the effect.

Matte paper, glossy paper, waxed paper

Different paper surface affects the brightness of printing ink, coarse surface paper will absorb the surface of the ink, there is basically no reflection sheen. Coarse paper is used to print text, such as newspapers, books, etc.

Paints and polishes

The role of coatings and polishes is to protect and enhance the ink area effect.
