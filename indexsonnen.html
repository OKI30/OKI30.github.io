Meet the Artist!

SONNENZIMMER

Sonnenzimmer is an experimental graphic studio founded by artists Nick Butcher and Nadine Nakanishi in 2006. Our collaborative work explores the historic role of the graphic mark and the contemporary graphic impulse through publishing, exhibitions, design, and performance. While we work in an array of media, our focus is on triangulating a deeper understanding of graphic expression at large. In addition to our self-driven work, we actively engage in commissioned projects aiming to reshape preconceived notions of the graphic arts. Our work has been shown in The United States, Brazil, China, and Europe; with recent solo exhibitions at Vebikus Kunsthalle Schaffhausen, Switzerland, and Hatch Show Print in Nashville, Tennessee.

As working graphic artists, we aim to continually build new partnerships with individuals, initiatives, and cultural producers. Our practice relies on the interplay between commissioned projects and our own exhibition and publishing efforts. As a studio, we thrive where expression, service, experimentation, and form collide. When we are all ready to look at our own graphic mark, own up to how it looks, learn to embrace it, feel compelled to live with it, the world will start to look like home for everybody.






How to Begin, Document gives tangible form to ideas explored in our recent experimental instructional video, How to Begin. Pulling from imagery developed for the short, the print seeks to document the fleeting moment between seeing and feeling and close the gap between thought and action. The multi-color screen print pushes and pulls between the here and now, and now and then merging the aesthetics of our earliest poster work with our recent explorations of motion graphics.
